LAS VEGAS VS. LOS ANGELES: CHOOSING YOUR CREW

Shooting in Vegas or LA? Here's how the two markets differ for video production, and a simple way to figure out which one your project actually needs.

Kyle ZuelkeMay 5, 2026 · 6 min read

Vegas and LA are a four-hour drive apart, but they're built for different kinds of production. Las Vegas runs on sports, live events, and conventions — Los Angeles runs on entertainment and brand-industry volume. Z Squared crews both markets regularly, and the honest answer to "which one do I need" almost always comes down to what kind of content you're producing, not which city sounds more glamorous.

What makes the Las Vegas market different?

Vegas is a live-event town. Between the Strip's resort venues, Allegiant Stadium, T-Mobile Arena, the Michelob Ultra Arena, and the Las Vegas Convention Center, this city hosts an outsized volume of sports broadcasts, fights, residencies, and trade shows relative to its size. We've crewed NFL Network's Super Bowl LVIII coverage at Allegiant, WNBA broadcasts for ESPN, and a steady stream of convention floor work — the market is dense with live, one-shot production that needs a crew comfortable working fast under broadcast conditions.

What makes the Los Angeles market different?

LA is entertainment and brand-industry density — studios, agencies, and a production culture built around longer-form and more polished deliverables, alongside its own share of major sports venues. We've crewed SoFi Stadium for NBA and NFL broadcasts, Crypto.com Arena, Hollywood Park, and documentary and brand work across Inglewood, Long Beach, Beverly Hills, Downtown LA, Pasadena, and Santa Monica. It's a market where agency and brand clients expect a certain production polish, and where documentary-style, multi-day engagements are more common than the one-day live hit that defines a lot of Vegas work.

Does it matter that Z Squared is based in Vegas but works in LA often?

In practice, not much — and that's by design. We're headquartered in Las Vegas, but LA is on our standing rotation; most weeks include at least one LA shoot. Because the drive is about four hours, we van our full kit in rather than flying — the same two Sony FX9s, Arri Amira, and full lighting and audio package that go to Allegiant Stadium go to SoFi. There's no sub-rental layer and no airline gear restrictions shrinking what shows up on set. For single-day LA shoots we go in and out same-day; for multi-day work we set up a temporary base in LA for the run of the project. See our Los Angeles location page for more on how that works.

Does it cost more to shoot in one market versus the other?

Travel is the main variable, not the crew itself. Because the drive between Vegas and LA is about four hours by van rather than a flight, there's no airline gear surcharge and no need to sub-rent equipment on arrival — the same owned kit that works a Vegas shoot works an LA one. A single-day LA shoot goes in and out same-day; a multi-day engagement means setting up a temporary LA base for the run of the project. Either way, the day-rate structure for the crew itself doesn't change between markets — what changes is the travel and logistics layer built around it, which gets quoted specifically for the project.

Can the same crew really work both markets well?

This is less about geography than about whether the crew has actually put in the hours in both places. A crew that's only ever shot in one market can find itself relearning venue logistics, local permitting quirks, and load-in routines every time it crosses into unfamiliar territory. Z Squared's answer to that is repetition — enough shoots at SoFi Stadium and enough shoots at Allegiant Stadium that neither one requires a learning curve anymore. That familiarity is what actually makes working two markets viable instead of just theoretically possible.

How do you decide which market's crew fits your shoot?

A few questions tend to sort this out quickly:

  • Is this a live sports broadcast, fight, or residency show? That's Vegas territory.
  • Is this a convention or trade show floor shoot? Vegas hosts more of these than almost anywhere in the country.
  • Is this brand, agency, or entertainment-industry content with a longer production timeline? LA's industry density often fits better.
  • Is this a multi-day documentary engagement? Either market works, but LA's talent and location density is a common draw.
  • Do you need a crew that already knows the venue? We've worked both Allegiant and SoFi enough times that load-in isn't a guessing game in either building.

Does crew quality actually differ between the two?

Not with Z Squared — it's the same crew, the same owned gear, and the same Emmy-winning DP oversight whether the call sheet says Las Vegas or Los Angeles. What differs is the surrounding market: the venues, the client mix, and the type of production that dominates each city's calendar. Choosing a market isn't about choosing a better crew, it's about matching your project to the ecosystem that's built around that kind of work.

If your project leans toward live events, sports, or convention-floor production, our post on why Las Vegas is a live event production hub goes deeper into what makes this city's calendar so packed. And if a trade show is part of the picture, trade show video production in Las Vegas covers the specific logistics of shooting on a show floor.

Whichever market fits, the practical advice is the same: describe the shoot, the location, and the timeline, and let the crew tell you which market's approach — live and fast, or polished and longer-form — actually serves the project best.
